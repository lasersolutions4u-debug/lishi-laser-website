How It Works

What is a Mixed Gas Device?

Compress N₂ and O₂ in a precise ratio to produce a mixed gas optimized for high-speed laser cutting — with micro-oxygen content that eliminates burrs while maximizing speed.

⚡ Technical Specifications

Input: N₂ + O₂ at 20–25 bar. Output: N₂ purity ~95%, pressure 12–16 bar, max flow 200m³/h. Compact gas tank (capacity >3m³).

📐 Device Dimensions

800mm × 350mm × 1100mm, weight 90kg. One device supports two laser machines simultaneously (One-to-Two configuration).

🎯 Cutting Effect

High cutting speed + zero burrs. After installation, adjust ratio between N₂ 95% and O₂ 5% for different board thicknesses. Built-in program analyzes usage ratio and records gas purity requirements.

🌍 Wide Compatibility

Works with all major brands: HAN'S, DNE, PENTA, LEAD, HSG, and more. Compatible with BODOR, KIMLA, MESSER, and other global brands.

Competitive Edge

Why Choose Mixed Gas Over O₂, N₂ or Air?

13 years of laser cutting industry experience. 160+ end users. The most stable mixed gas device manufacturer in China.

🚀

3× Faster Cutting

Compared to oxygen cutting. Carbon steel cutting speed is up to 3× faster. Example: 8mm CS at 16m/min vs 2–3m/min with O₂.

✨

Zero Burrs

Unlike nitrogen cutting which produces burrs on plates ≥8mm (12KW) or ≥10mm (20KW), mixed gas cutting produces clean, burr-free surfaces.

💨

Less Gas Consumption

Compared to nitrogen, mixed gas reduces consumption by 33–50%. Similar total consumption to O₂ but far superior speed.

⚡

Ultra Low Power

Power consumption: only 2 kWh / 24 hours. Far less than air compressors which require constant maintenance every 3,000 hours.

🔧

One-to-Two Support

The only manufacturer with stable one-to-two mixed gas equipment — one device powers two laser machines of different power levels.

🌐

Protected Gas Pipeline

Unlike air compressors that contaminate gas pipelines and damage laser head lenses, mixed gas keeps your optical system safe.
