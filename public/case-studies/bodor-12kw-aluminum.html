The Challenge

A fabricator producing aluminum enclosures, brackets, and panels runs a BODOR 12kW fiber laser. Aluminum is notoriously difficult to cut clean — it's reflective, thermally conductive, and prone to burr formation. Their existing pure nitrogen setup produced acceptable edges but at speeds that limited daily output.

  • Laser: BODOR 12kW fiber laser
  • Material: Aluminum sheet (1.5mm–8mm)
  • Products: Electronic enclosures, mounting brackets, decorative panels
  • Previous gas: Pure nitrogen — clean edges but slow, high gas consumption
  • Quality requirement: Cosmetic surfaces — no burrs, no scratches, no secondary finishing

The Solution: Mixed Gas Optimized for Aluminum

EUCHIO Mixed Gas configured a mixed gas station with an aluminum-optimized N₂/O₂ ratio. For aluminum, the微量氧 component helps stabilize the cutting front against aluminum's high thermal conductivity, while the nitrogen主导 blend prevents oxidation that would ruin cosmetic surfaces.

The result: faster cuts than pure nitrogen, cleaner edges with zero burrs, and 35% lower gas cost — because mixed gas uses far less total gas volume than pure N₂ for the same cut length.

Key Takeaways

Aluminum Without Compromise

Mixed gas solves the classic aluminum dilemma: pure O₂ is fast but oxidizes the surface; pure N₂ is clean but slow. The optimized blend delivers both — speed and cosmetic quality in one gas stream.

35% Lower Gas Cost

Mixed gas uses significantly less total volume than pure nitrogen for the same cut length. The fabricator saw a 35% reduction in monthly gas expenses while increasing throughput.

BODOR Plug-and-Play

Zero modifications to the BODOR 12kW. The mixing station connected to the existing gas inlet in under 30 minutes. Same laser, same operator, same nesting software — just faster, cleaner cuts.
