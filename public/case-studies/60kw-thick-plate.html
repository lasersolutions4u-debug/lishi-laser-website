The Challenge

A structural steel fabricator producing heavy machinery components needed to cut carbon steel plates from 30mm to 50mm thickness. Their existing process used pure nitrogen on a 60kW fiber laser — edge quality was acceptable but cutting speed was a bottleneck. Worse, parts above 40mm often required post-cut grinding before welding.

  • Laser: 60kW fiber laser
  • Material: Carbon steel (30mm–50mm)
  • Application: Heavy machinery structural components
  • Previous gas: Pure nitrogen — slow on thick plate, inconsistent edge quality
  • Pain point: Post-cut grinding on 40mm+ plates was eating 2–3 hours per shift

The Solution: Mixed Gas for High-Power Thick Plate

EUCHIO Mixed Gas configured a mixed gas station tuned specifically for high-power thick-plate cutting. At 60kW, the precise N₂/O₂ blend generates an optimized cutting front temperature — hot enough to melt through 50mm plate efficiently, with enough nitrogen to protect the kerf from oxidation.

The result: clean, silver-white edges across all thicknesses. Parts go directly from the laser bed to welding — the grinding station was reassigned to other work.

Key Takeaways

Thick Plate Without Compromise

Mixed gas makes 50mm carbon steel cuts that are clean enough to skip grinding entirely. The N₂ component protects the kerf while O₂ delivers the cutting energy — best of both worlds at high power.

Speed Gap Widens with Thickness

The speed advantage vs. pure nitrogen grows as plate thickness increases: 1.4x at 20mm, 2.1x at 50mm. The thicker the plate, the more mixed gas outperforms.

Eliminated 2-3 hrs/day Grinding

Post-cut machining was the hidden cost of pure N₂ thick-plate cutting. Mixed gas eliminated it entirely — saving 10–15 hours of labor per week and freeing the grinding station for other work.
