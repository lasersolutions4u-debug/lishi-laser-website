The Challenge

A metal fabrication shop in Southeast Asia was running four BODOR 20kW fiber lasers on pure oxygen to cut carbon steel. Their main product line required 6mm black steel sheet, and every part needed grinding before it could be welded or painted.

  • Cutting speed on 6mm: 2.3 m/min — they thought this was normal
  • Edge quality: Heavy oxidation, rough surface, discoloration
  • Grinding department: 6 full-time workers, every shift
  • Monthly gas bill: ~$3,200 (liquid oxygen, bulk delivery)
  • Rework rate: ~12% of parts rejected due to grinding errors

The Solution

EUCHIO Mixed Gas installed two mixed gas stations in One-to-Three mode — each station feeding two BODOR lasers simultaneously. The system mixes liquid nitrogen with a precise 5% oxygen blend using IGBT-controlled metering valves (±0.5% accuracy).

  • Installation: 2 mixing stations → 4 lasers (one-to-three per station)
  • Setup time: 3 days including on-site training
  • Weekly consumption: ~3,200L LN₂ + ~170L LO₂ (all 4 machines)
  • No modifications to the BODOR lasers — plug-and-play with existing gas inlets
